Space Mission Launches

from 1957 - 2020

The artificial staellite Sputnik 8K71PS, more commonly known as Sputnik-1 was the first man made object to venture into outer space. Raketnye Voiska Strategicheskogo Naznacheniya (RVSN USSR) was responsible for the launch. This mission marked the beginning of the great space race and the journey for human beings to explore the universe beyond the limits of our planet.

Global Space Mission Launches per Country

At the beginning of the space race the United States of America and the Russia, then the USSR, were in a technological feud. Each was racing to be the first to put humans in space and eventually on the moon. The number of launches in these countries is by far the highest, Russia's total missions totalling 1398 and the USA 1349.

Space Mission Rocket and Satellite Status

As time progresses older rockets are retired from service and newer rockets take there place. This stacked bar graph to the right illustrates that almost all of the rockets and satellites launches before 1991 are retired while only 1 rocket launched in 2020 has been retired.

Top 5 Space Mission Launch Companies Over Time

The 5 companies with the greatest number of launches are the RVSN USSR, NASA (National Aeronautics and Space Administration), Arianespace, General Dynamics and CASC (China Aerospace Science and Technology Corporation). Competition drove the USSR and the USA to increased numbers of launches in the early days of space exploration but as time progressed the number of launches significantly reduced. Since the 1990's Arianespace (French Multinational) and CASC have been dominating the number of launches and the RVSN USSR has since dissolved.

Mission Spending and Success

Space mission launches are a high-risk, high-reward operation with many opportunities for errors. There are three failure classifications: Overall Failure, Partial Failure and Prelaunch Failure. Millions of dollars and lives at risk, hence failure rates are low. Initially the failure rate was 70%-80% but improvements have been made and was only 9% in 2020.

The relationship between mission spending and success rate is weak. The USA spends 28 times more on per space launch on average than the smallest spender, New Zealand, but experiences only a 3.3% increase in success. Countries with similar or greater success rates spend between $32-$47mil per launch.
Global Launch Sites

Each country generally performs space mission launches on home soil from one or many launch sites. Cape Canaveral (USA), Plesetsk Cosmodrome (Russia) and Baikonur Cosmodrome (Khazakstan) are the most extensively used sites, globally. France is an exceptional case, their launches are performed from a French/European space port in French Guiana (South America).

Successes and Failures of Each Launch Site

Launch sites experience varying degrees of success.

Launch Days and Times

In UTC (Coordinated Universal Time) the most common time for launches to occur is Wednesday between 09:00 and 10:00. Launches rarely occur on Sundays and Mondays or in the very early hours of the morning.
